Match Recap

IPL 2026 · Match 11 · Bengaluru · April 5
RCB 250/3 beat CSK 207 by 43 runs
RCB 250/3 CSK 207 (19.4 ov)

CSK asked RCB to bat on a sticky Chinnaswamy pitch — smart call for 45 overs. Then Tim David walked in at 151/3 in the 15th over and rewrote the script entirely.

David and Patidar shared 44 balls between them, hitting 14 sixes. David's 19th-over burst off Overton (6,4,6,6,6 = 30 runs from one over) sent the Chinnaswamy into frenzy. David finished on 70* off 25 balls. Patidar contributed 48* off 19. Padikkal earlier anchored with a composed 50 off 29 in a sticky first half.

CSK's chase never recovered after losing 3 wickets inside 3 overs. Sarfaraz Khan blazed 50 off 24 but was stumped immediately after the powerplay. Prashant Veer showed spark with 43 off 29, while Bhuvneshwar Kumar claimed his landmark 200th IPL wicket (3/41). It wasn't enough. CSK slid to their third successive defeat.

⚡ Turning Point: Tim David's arrival in over 15. The match changed in real time — 91/1 after 10 overs became 250/3 by the end.
IPL 2026 · Match 10 · Hyderabad · April 5
LSG 160/5 beat SRH 156/9 by 5 wickets (with 1 ball remaining)
SRH 156/9 LSG 160/5 (19.5 ov)

Mohammed Shami vs his former team. In 4 clinical powerplay overs, he dismissed Abhishek and Travis Head cheaply with cutters — SRH were a wreck at 26/4 in the 8th over.

What followed was extraordinary. Heinrich Klaasen (62) and Nitish Kumar Reddy (56) added a 116-run 5th-wicket stand — SRH's highest ever for that wicket — to drag them to 156. In the chase, LSG stumbled mid-innings (Pooran run out, Badoni stumped) leaving Pant to do it the hard way.

9 needed off 6 balls. Pant — laboured and strained all innings on 68-ball crawl — calmly hit two fours off the first two deliveries of the last over to level scores, then sealed it with a loft over mid-off. That's captaincy.

⚡ Turning Point: Shami's economy in overs 1–6. Setting up 26/4 made 156 chaseable before LSG had faced a ball.

Data Insight of the Day

1. CSK's Death Bowling Crisis

In all three IPL 2026 games so far, CSK have conceded 60+ runs in overs 17–20. Opponents are deliberately targeting this weakness — and it's working every single time.

💡 Think of it like a leaky tap: annoying in isolation, catastrophic across a season. Without Deepak Chahar or a settled death specialist, CSK's auction gaps are becoming match results.

2. Klaasen & Reddy — SRH's Lifeline Partnership

In two consecutive games, this pair have added 82 runs (Game 1) and 116 runs (Game 2) after SRH's top order collapsed under 30/4. They are the entire SRH middle order right now — everyone else is a spectator.

💡 Like a car running on 2 of 6 cylinders — moving, but not sustainably. Head, Kishan, and Livingstone must fire together at some point.

Cricket Intelligence

The Shami Masterclass — Front-Loading Your Best Bowler

Shami took 2/9 tonight, but the wickets don't tell the full story. He bowled 3 of his 4 overs in the powerplay against SRH — his former team — using cutters and offcutters (not swing) to deceive batters expecting raw pace. LSG didn't save him for the death. They used him up front, killed the game at 26/4, then let lesser bowlers defend the total with momentum already in hand.

Most teams save their best pacer for overs 17–20. LSG's strategy — front-loading on a seam-friendly morning pitch against a fragile top order — is increasingly the smarter call. The trend in T20 strategy is shifting: when conditions favour pace early, use your weapon then. Don't wait.

Women's Cricket — Fun Fact

🏟️

Largest crowd ever for a women's sporting event in Australia

The 2020 ICC Women's T20 World Cup Final at the Melbourne Cricket Ground drew a staggering 86,174 fans — the largest attendance ever recorded for a women's cricket match worldwide, and the biggest crowd for any women's sporting event in Australian history. Australia beat India by 85 runs in front of a sold-out MCG. The match was played on International Women's Day, making the occasion even more iconic. For context, that crowd was bigger than many men's Test match attendances at the same ground.
